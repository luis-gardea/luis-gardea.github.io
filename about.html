About me...

Currently a sophomore at Stanford University, I am pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science. I am from El Paso, Texas, which lies right on the border of The United States and Mexico. I have very broad interests ranging from music and playing the guitar to video games and PC gaming to watching movies from esteemed Mexican directors to playing Ultimate Frisbee with friends.

Originally intending on Electrical Engineering, my classes at Stanford have greatly sparked my interest in computer programming and software. Thus, I have so far taken, CS 106A and 106B (Programming Methodology and Abstractions), CS 107 (Computer Organization and Systems), and CS 110 (Principles of Computer Systems) as part of the programming CS core at Stanford. I have also taken CS 103 (Mathematical Methods of Computing) and CS 109 (Computer Science Probability) as part of the mathematics part of the CS core. Currently, I am enrolled in CS 205A which covers numerical analysis and linear algebra, as well as the mathematical methods used in vision, graphics and machine learning, and CS 161, which focuses on mathematical analysis and design of algorithms.
